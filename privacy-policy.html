Privacy Policy

Last updated: 26 June 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Eponymical Games Ltd ("we", "us", "our") collects, uses and protects your personal data when you visit our website or use our apps, including Learn Piano: Play Songs.

Learn Piano: Play Songs is currently in pre-release/TestFlight preparation. Some features and data practices described here apply to the website and early-access waitlist today, and to the app once it launches or is tested.

1. Who we are

Eponymical Games Ltd is a private limited company registered in England & Wales, based in London, United Kingdom. For the purposes of UK data protection law (UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018), we are the data controller for the personal data described in this policy.

You can contact us at any time at direct@eponymicalgames.com.

2. Information we collect

  • Information you give us — such as your name, email address, waitlist signup, support messages, and any details you include in a message.
  • Account information — if you create an account or sign in, we may process your email address, display name, sign-in provider identifier, and authentication identifiers from providers such as Apple, Google, Facebook and Firebase.
  • Learning and app content — in-app progress such as songs played, arrangements, levels completed, scores, streaks, saved settings, and imported learning material. User-selected audio files and microphone input are used to provide piano practice and analysis features.
  • Microphone and audio data — microphone input is used for pitch detection during practice. The app is designed to analyze microphone input on the device for live note detection. User-selected files are used for song import and transcription features; if you choose to use a cloud or third-party analysis endpoint, the selected file or derived analysis data may be sent to the configured service.
  • Purchase and subscription data — if paid features are enabled, Apple and RevenueCat may provide us with product identifiers, transaction identifiers, subscription status, entitlement status, renewal/cancellation information, and anonymous app user identifiers. We do not receive your full payment card details.
  • Advertising, analytics and attribution data — we may process app events, crash logs, device identifiers, advertising identifiers, consent status, approximate region, IP address, campaign attribution signals, SKAdNetwork postback data, ad impressions/clicks and related diagnostic data. The Identifier for Advertisers (IDFA) is collected only if you grant permission through Apple's App Tracking Transparency prompt.
  • Technical data — device model, operating system, app version, browser information, push notification tokens, IP address, approximate region, diagnostics and performance data collected automatically to keep our services secure and working.

3. How we use your information

  • To send you launch news and early-access updates you've asked for.
  • To respond to your enquiries and provide support.
  • To operate, maintain and improve our website and apps.
  • To personalise your learning experience, sync your progress where enabled, and track your practice history.
  • To process purchases, subscriptions, entitlements, restores, refunds and billing support.
  • To request notification permission and send app notifications where you enable them.
  • To measure advertising performance, prevent duplicate purchase reporting, and improve acquisition campaigns.
  • To keep our services secure and to detect and prevent abuse.
  • To comply with our legal obligations.

4. Legal bases for processing

We rely on the following legal bases under UK GDPR: your consent (for marketing emails, optional app permissions, tracking permission, and certain cookies or personalised advertising where required); contract (to provide the app, account features, purchases and support you request); our legitimate interests (to run, secure, debug and improve our services, measure non-personalised performance, and prevent abuse); and legal obligation (where the law requires us to process or retain data).

5. Sharing your information

We do not sell your personal data. We share it only with trusted service providers who help us operate our services, and only as needed to perform those services on our behalf or as required by law.

  • Apple for App Store distribution, TestFlight, in-app purchases, subscriptions, Sign in with Apple, push notification infrastructure and App Tracking Transparency controls.
  • Google/Firebase for authentication, analytics, Crashlytics diagnostics, Firestore cloud sync, Firebase Cloud Messaging and advertising/consent SDKs such as Google Mobile Ads and User Messaging Platform.
  • RevenueCat for purchase validation, subscription entitlement management and app-store purchase analytics.
  • Meta/Facebook for optional login, app events, advertising attribution, SKAdNetwork-related campaign measurement and ad mediation where enabled.
  • GitHub Pages and FormSubmit for website hosting and contact/waitlist form delivery.
  • AI or analysis providers only if you choose to enable a configurable cloud analysis endpoint or similar feature in the app.

6. Data retention

We keep your personal data only for as long as necessary for the purposes set out above, or as required by law. Account, support and purchase records may be retained where needed for security, dispute handling, tax, accounting or app-store compliance. You can ask us to delete your data at any time, although some app-store transaction records may need to be retained by Apple, RevenueCat or us where legally required.

7. Your rights

Under UK data protection law you have the right to access, correct, delete or restrict the processing of your personal data, to object to processing, and to data portability. To exercise any of these rights, email us at direct@eponymicalgames.com. You also have the right to complain to the UK Information Commissioner's Office (ICO).

8. Children's privacy

Our services are designed to be suitable for a general audience but are not directed at children under the age where parental consent is required by applicable law. Where a user is a child, we expect a parent or guardian to manage their use, permissions and any sign-up. We do not knowingly collect more personal data from children than is necessary, and we will delete such data on request.

9. Cookies & similar technologies

Our website may use a small number of cookies and similar technologies to remember your preferences, deliver forms and measure usage. The app may use SDK identifiers and similar technologies for authentication, analytics, crash reporting, purchases, advertising measurement and consent management. You can control website cookies through your browser settings, app tracking through iOS settings, and app permissions such as microphone or notifications through your device settings.

10. International transfers

Some of our service providers may process data outside the UK. Where that happens, we take steps to ensure your data is protected by appropriate safeguards as required by law.

11. Security

We use appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your personal data. No method of transmission or storage is completely secure, but we work hard to safeguard your information.

12. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we do, we'll revise the "last updated" date above and, where appropriate, let you know.
